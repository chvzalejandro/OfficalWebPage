Community

Throughout my college career at Sacramento State University, I spent a lot of time participating in clubs. I became a member of SHPE (Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers) which not only helped me immensely, but also allowed me to give back to the community. Through SHPE I was able to participate in events that allowed us to go to local schools and explain them to what it means to be a STEM major. To break down basic principles in engineering and explaining them in a manner that anyone could understand it.

I was also a vice-president for IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) and helped grow the club. As vice-president it was my duty to support the club president by ensuring that we stayed focused on our goals. I was also in charge of growing the club. We worked diligently to provide the students with a club that not only supported them, but also provided a community for them to grow with.
